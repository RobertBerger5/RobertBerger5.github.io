DNA Evidence

How can genetic sequencing and molecular genetics support human evolution?

The genetic difference between humans, chimpanzees and the chimps cousin the bonobo is only 1.2%. Also, the difference between the human and gorilla genome is only 1.6%. Due to the close relation of humans and chimps it is know that we shared a common ancestor that lived around 6 to 8 million years ago. From this common ancestor there was the split of the human lineage and the present day Chimpanzee. Also due to the close relation of humans and apes humans are classified in the family called “Great Apes” (as you can see below).

Neanderthals

About 40,000 years ago homo sapiens and neanderthals inhabited Europe together. Nearly 5,000 years later, neanderthals were no longer a part of the Earth. Because of these known facts, it is believed that the homo sapiens played a large role in the destruction of the neanderthals. However the exact way the humans did it is unclear. The idea that homo sapiens won against the neanderthals in a war isn’t true due to the fact that neanderthals had vastly superior strength. One more reasonable option is that the homo sapiens, who were smarter, were able to gather all the resources available to the land. Without food or water to support the neanderthals larger bodies, they quickly started to die out.

Neanderthals were much like homo sapiens in their appearance, and close in their intellect. The only thing differencing the two was the fact that homo sapiens had developed frontal lobes. This allowed them to create a language, where the neanderthals didn’t have the brain capacity to do so. Neanderthals had very large eyes and large bodies, so their brains were devoted almost solely to movement and sight.

Mitochondria

Only women can pass on mitochondrial DNA and by tracing it back through human history, it stops at one woman in Africa who is thought to be the mother of all homo sapiens. This is why humans are thought to originate from Africa because there are no other traces dating back farther than that woman in Africa. To answer the question of different skin colors, it is believed that since people in Africa have darker skin and the sun shines brighter and longer than most other places in the world, that their skin develops melanin which protects against ultraviolet light and skin cancers.
