Who were the ancestors of modern man?

Homo sapiens and Neanderthals share a common ancestor that probably lived in Africa more than half a million years ago. The ancestors of Neanderthals were the first to move to Europe and Asia while the modern-human lineage stayed in Africa. But after modern humans began to leave Africa less than 100,000 years ago, they interbred with the Neanderthals who had settled on a range stretching from Western Europe to Siberia.

Homonoid Groups

  • Sahelanthropus tchadensis

    • Found: 2001
    • Where: Toros-Manalla site 266, Chad
    • Temporal Range: 7 to 6 million years ago
    • Walking upright
    • Lived in forests and grasslands
    • Small brain
    • Spinal chord opening underneath the skull

  • Ardipithecus ramidus

    • Found: December 1992
    • Where: Aramis, Ethiopia
    • Temporal Range: 4.5 to 4.2 million years ago
    • Large toe combined with a rigid foot
    • The pelvis said to show adaptations combining tree-climbing and bipedal activity
    • Height: average 3 ft 11 inches
    • Weight: average 110 lbs

  • Australopithecus africanus

    • Found: October 1924
    • Where: Taung also Sterkfontein and Makapansgat, South Africa
    • Temporal Range: 3 to 2 million years ago
    • Had rounder craniums than earlier human-like mammals, with bigger brain but smaller teeth
    • Pelvis and foot bones show it walked bipedal but shoulder and hand bones show it still was adapted to climb

  • Homo erectus

    • Found: October 1891
    • Where: Trinil, Indonesia
    • Temporal Range: 1.8 million to 50 thousand years ago
    • Brain capacity: 1000 cubic cm.
    • Thinner skull bones
    • Used fire

  • Homo neanderthalensis

    • Found: August 1856
    • Where: Feldhofer, Neander Valley, Germany also Europe generally, Central Asia
    • Temporal Range: 200 to 28 thousand years ago
    • Brain Capacity: 1500 cubic cm
    • Buried their dead
    • Possibility of having a language
